Understanding Democracy

How Elections Work

The U.S. election process involves multiple stages that ensure fair representation and democratic participation.

Voter Registration

Citizens must register to vote before participating. Requirements vary by state, with deadlines typically 15-30 days before an election.

Primary Elections

Parties hold primaries and caucuses to select candidates from February through June of the election year.

National Conventions

Each party holds a convention where delegates officially nominate their presidential candidate and adopt the party platform.

General Election

Held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in November, citizens cast their votes for President and other offices.

Electoral College

The President is formally elected by 538 electors. A candidate needs 270 electoral votes to win.

Inauguration

On January 20th, the President-elect takes the oath of office and officially begins their term.
